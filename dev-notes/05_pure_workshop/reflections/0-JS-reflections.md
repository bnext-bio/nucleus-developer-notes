# Day 1 - small molecule reflection
No sodium, yes potassiun. This includes less obvious elements like using KOH to buffer, not NaOH. 
The acid side is less clear (mostly because HEPES, NTPs, AAs are all acids that need base), but glacial acetic acid is a safe bet.

# Day 2 - protein mix reflection
No shame in heaviliy increasing the washes. It is known that extra contaminating proteins will come off the column (these are unique to one-pot, although 36-pot is also not guaranteed to be clean either). Imidazole can kill PURE, protein dialysis is going to be very important after eluting from the column. Diluting/Concentrating may not be enough to get the buffer clean enough

Determining purity is unfortunately difficult as many of the proteins have very close MWs so it is difficult to establish a easily identifiable banding pattern to point to contaminants

# Day 3 - rescue the protein mix by drop ins
Ribosomes strike me as the *easiest** component to make 
*do not confuse easy with easiest

Protein spike ins are a potentially very helpful, but dangerous game to play. Combinatorial explosion is always ready to rear its head
# Day 4 - rescue the protein mix by dialysis
Based on partial data from tRNA and protein dialysis, it seems o/n steps can be your friend when used well especially when considering the desire to avoid freeze-thaw cycles and prepare all components on a similar timeline.
